OUR HISTORY

113 years as a beacon of education in Ijebuland

The story of the school the platform is built around — and why connecting every generation of it matters.

FROM 1913 TO TODAY

A school built by faith, sustained by its old boys

1892

Western education takes root in Ijebuland

Decades after Christianity reached Lagos and Abeokuta, the Ijebu people openly embraced the faith — and with it, the missionary push for Western-style schooling.

20 January 1913

Ijebu-Ode Grammar School is founded

Rev. S.J. Gansallo, with support from Rev. J.A. Cole, opened the school under the Church Missionary Society with just fourteen pioneering students — making it the first secondary school in Ijebuland.

1919

A new chapter in leadership

Rev. Israel Oludotun Ransome-Kuti, already known for his work mobilising old students elsewhere, took up the principalship — a reminder that strong alumni networks and strong school leadership have always gone together here.

Today

113 years on, still the dawn of life

JOGS remains the oldest secondary school in Ijebuland and one of the oldest in Ogun State. JOGSOBA, the old boys' association, is active at home and abroad, including a standing chapter in North America.

THE MOTTO

Non Nobis Domine

“Not unto us, O Lord”

A reminder that what the school has built — and what this platform tries to extend — isn't for any one set or any one office. It's meant to outlast all of us, the same way the school has.

WHY THIS PLATFORM

One record of the school, instead of three disconnected ones

Right now, the school's administration, its old boys, and its current students each keep their own version of "what's happening at JOGS." This platform is an attempt to make that one shared, living record — while still giving the school administration the final say over what gets published.

For the administration

One place to publish news, manage admissions information, and see who from JOGSOBA wants to give back — without needing a developer for routine updates.

For old boys

A proper directory by set and year, a way to find classmates, and a structured way to mentor, hire, or give back to the school that shaped them.

For students

Subject resources and past questions in one place, and direct access to the kind of mentorship that, until now, only happened by chance during a reunion visit.
